01

Charged particle in a solenoid’s axial field

JEE Main 2020

Skill chain: solenoid field → radius of circular motion → the “farthest point is a diameter” geometry → the no-collision condition \(2r\le R\).

Fig 1 · Orbit launched from the axis passes through the start point — its farthest reach is the diameter 2r.
L1Easy

A long solenoid has \(n\) turns per unit length and carries a current \(I\). Write the magnitude of the magnetic field on its axis.

▸ Solution

The field inside a long solenoid is uniform and axial: \(B=\mu_0 n I\).

\(B=\mu_0 n I\)
L2Warm-up

An electron (charge \(e\), mass \(m\)) moves with speed \(v\) perpendicular to a uniform field \(B\). Find the radius of its circular path.

▸ Solution

Magnetic force supplies the centripetal force: \(evB=\dfrac{mv^2}{r}\).

\(r=\dfrac{mv}{eB}\)
L3Core

An electron is launched from a point \(O\), moving perpendicular to a uniform field, and traces a circle of radius \(r\). What is the maximum distance of the electron from \(O\)?

▸ Solution

The circular orbit passes through the launch point \(O\). The point farthest from \(O\) on that circle is the diametrically opposite point.

Maximum distance \(=2r\) (a diameter)
L4Challenge

An electron is shot radially (perpendicular to the axis) from the axis of a long solenoid of radius \(R\), where the axial field is \(B\). Find the maximum speed \(v\) so it never strikes the wall, in terms of \(e,m,B,R\).

▸ Solution

Farthest reach from the axis is \(2r\). No collision requires \(2r\le R\):

\[2\cdot\frac{mv}{eB}\le R \;\Rightarrow\; v\le \frac{eBR}{2m}\]

\(v_{\max}=\dfrac{eBR}{2m}\)
L5Original · JEE Main 2020

An electron gun on the axis of a long solenoid (radius \(R\), \(n\) turns/length, current \(I\)) shoots an electron radially with speed \(v\). If the electron does not hit the solenoid surface, the maximum possible \(v\) is:

▸ Solution

Substitute the solenoid field \(B=\mu_0 n I\) into \(v_{\max}=\dfrac{eBR}{2m}\):

\[v_{\max}=\frac{e\,(\mu_0 n I)\,R}{2m}\]

Answer (3)   \(\dfrac{e\mu_0 n I R}{2m}\)
02

Pitch of a helical path

JEE Main 2020

Skill chain: period \(T=\frac{2\pi m}{qB}\) → resolve \(v\) into \(v_\parallel,v_\perp\) → pitch \(=v_\parallel T\) → full numeric.

Fig 2 · One pitch = distance advanced along B in one revolution = \(v_\parallel\,T\).
L1Easy

A proton (mass \(m\), charge \(q\)) moves perpendicular to a uniform field \(B\). Write the period \(T\) of its circular motion.

▸ Solution

From \(r=\frac{mv}{qB}\) and \(T=\frac{2\pi r}{v}\), the speed cancels:

\(T=\dfrac{2\pi m}{qB}\) — independent of \(v\)
L2Warm-up

A proton enters a field \(B\) with speed \(v\) at angle \(\theta\) to \(B\). Write its velocity components parallel and perpendicular to \(B\).

▸ Solution

\(v_\parallel\) drives the forward drift (no force); \(v_\perp\) drives the circle.

\(v_\parallel=v\cos\theta,\quad v_\perp=v\sin\theta\)
L3Core

The pitch is the axial distance covered in one revolution. If \(v_\parallel=2\times10^{5}\,\text{m/s}\) and \(T=2\times10^{-7}\,\text{s}\), find the pitch.

▸ Solution

\[p=v_\parallel\,T=(2\times10^{5})(2\times10^{-7})=4\times10^{-2}\,\text{m}\]

\(p=4\text{ cm}\)
L4Challenge

A proton (\(m=1.67\times10^{-27}\,\text{kg}\), \(q=1.69\times10^{-19}\,\text{C}\)) with speed \(4\times10^{5}\,\text{m/s}\) enters \(B=0.3\,\text{T}\) at \(60^\circ\). Find the radius of the helix.

▸ Solution

Only \(v_\perp\) sets the radius: \(v_\perp=v\sin60^\circ=4\times10^5\times\tfrac{\sqrt3}{2}=3.46\times10^5\).

\[r=\frac{m v_\perp}{qB}=\frac{(1.67\times10^{-27})(3.46\times10^5)}{(1.69\times10^{-19})(0.3)}\approx1.14\times10^{-2}\,\text{m}\]

\(r\approx1.1\text{ cm}\)
L5Original · JEE Main 2020

A beam of protons with speed \(4\times10^{5}\,\text{m/s}\) enters \(B=0.3\,\text{T}\) at \(60^\circ\). The pitch of the resulting helix is close to: (options 2, 12, 5, 4 cm).

▸ Solution

Pitch uses the parallel component and the full period:

\[v_\parallel=v\cos60^\circ=2\times10^{5},\qquad T=\frac{2\pi m}{qB}=\frac{2\pi(1.67\times10^{-27})}{(1.69\times10^{-19})(0.3)}\approx2.07\times10^{-7}\,\text{s}\]

\[p=v_\parallel T=(2\times10^{5})(2.07\times10^{-7})\approx4.1\times10^{-2}\,\text{m}\]

Answer (4)   \(\approx 4\text{ cm}\)
03

Magnetic force via the cross product

JEE Main 2020

Skill chain: \(F=qvB\) → \(F=qvB\sin\theta\) → 2-D determinant → full 3-D \(\vec F=q\,\vec v\times\vec B\) with prefixes.

Fig 3 · \(\vec F=q\,\vec v\times\vec B\) is perpendicular to the plane of \(\vec v\) and \(\vec B\).
L1Easy

A charge \(q\) moves with speed \(v\) perpendicular to a uniform field \(B\). Give the magnitude of the magnetic force.

▸ Solution

With \(\theta=90^\circ\), \(\sin\theta=1\).

\(F=qvB\)
L2Warm-up

A \(2\,\mu\text{C}\) charge moves at \(3\times10^{4}\,\text{m/s}\) at \(30^\circ\) to a \(0.5\,\text{T}\) field. Find the force. What is it if the motion is parallel to \(B\)?

▸ Solution

\[F=qvB\sin\theta=(2\times10^{-6})(3\times10^{4})(0.5)(0.5)=1.5\times10^{-2}\,\text{N}\]

Parallel to \(B\): \(\sin0^\circ=0\Rightarrow F=0\).

\(F=1.5\times10^{-2}\,\text{N}\); zero when parallel
L3Core

A charge \(q=1\,\mu\text{C}\) moves with \(\vec v=(3\hat i+4\hat j)\,\text{m/s}\) in \(\vec B=2\hat k\,\text{T}\). Find \(\vec F\).

▸ Solution

\[\vec v\times\vec B=\begin{vmatrix}\hat i&\hat j&\hat k\\3&4&0\\0&0&2\end{vmatrix}=8\hat i-6\hat j\]

\(\vec F=q(\vec v\times\vec B)=10^{-6}(8\hat i-6\hat j)\).

\(\vec F=(8\hat i-6\hat j)\times10^{-6}\,\text{N}\)
L4Challenge

Compute the full 3-D cross product \(\vec v\times\vec B\) for \(\vec v=(\hat i+2\hat j+3\hat k)\) and \(\vec B=(2\hat i+\hat j+\hat k)\).

▸ Solution

\[\begin{vmatrix}\hat i&\hat j&\hat k\\1&2&3\\2&1&1\end{vmatrix} =\hat i(2\!-\!3)-\hat j(1\!-\!6)+\hat k(1\!-\!4)\]

\(\vec v\times\vec B=-\hat i+5\hat j-3\hat k\)
L5Original · JEE Main 2020

A charge \(1\,\mu\text{C}\) moves with \(\vec v=(2\hat i+3\hat j+4\hat k)\,\text{m/s}\) in \(\vec B=(5\hat i+3\hat j-6\hat k)\times10^{-3}\,\text{T}\). If \(\vec F=\vec F\times10^{-9}\,\text{N}\), find the vector.

▸ Solution

\[\vec v\times\vec B=\begin{vmatrix}\hat i&\hat j&\hat k\\2&3&4\\5&3&-6\end{vmatrix}\times10^{-3}=(-30\hat i+32\hat j-9\hat k)\times10^{-3}\]

\[\vec F=q(\vec v\times\vec B)=10^{-6}\times(-30\hat i+32\hat j-9\hat k)\times10^{-3}=(-30\hat i+32\hat j-9\hat k)\times10^{-9}\]

Answer (4)   \(-30\hat i+32\hat j-9\hat k\)
04

Magnetic force from an EM wave

JEE Main 2020

Skill chain: \(B_0=E_0/c\) → directions in \(E=E_0\hat j\sin(kx-\omega t)\) → \(F=quB_0\) → collapse to \(F=0.1\,qE_0\).

Fig 4 · \(E\parallel\hat y\), \(B\parallel\hat z\), wave travels along \(x\); the electron drifts along \(y\).
L1Easy

An EM wave has electric-field amplitude \(E_0=30\,\text{V/m}\). Find the magnetic-field amplitude \(B_0\). (\(c=3\times10^{8}\,\text{m/s}\))

▸ Solution

\[B_0=\frac{E_0}{c}=\frac{30}{3\times10^{8}}=1\times10^{-7}\,\text{T}\]

\(B_0=10^{-7}\,\text{T}\)
L2Warm-up

For \(\vec E=E_0\hat j\,\sin(kx-\omega t)\), state the direction of propagation and the direction of \(\vec B\).

▸ Solution

Phase \((kx-\omega t)\Rightarrow\) travels along \(+x\). Since \(\hat E\times\hat B=\) direction of propagation, \(\hat j\times\hat B=\hat i\Rightarrow\hat B=\hat k\).

Propagation \(+x\); \(\vec B\parallel\hat z\)
L3Core

A charge \(q=1.6\times10^{-19}\,\text{C}\) moves at \(3\times10^{7}\,\text{m/s}\) perpendicular to \(B_0=10^{-7}\,\text{T}\). Find the magnetic force.

▸ Solution

\[F=qvB_0=(1.6\times10^{-19})(3\times10^{7})(10^{-7})=4.8\times10^{-19}\,\text{N}\]

\(F=4.8\times10^{-19}\,\text{N}\)
L4Challenge

An electron moves along \(y\) with speed \(0.1c\) through a wave whose magnetic amplitude is \(B_0=10^{-7}\,\text{T}\) (with \(B\parallel z\)). Find the maximum magnetic force.

▸ Solution

\(v=0.1c=3\times10^{7}\); \(v\perp B\), so \[F=qvB_0=(1.6\times10^{-19})(3\times10^{7})(10^{-7})=4.8\times10^{-19}\,\text{N}\]

\(F=4.8\times10^{-19}\,\text{N}\)
L5Original · JEE Main 2020

An electron moves along \(y\) at \(0.1c\) in a wave \(\vec E=30\hat j\sin(1.5\times10^{7}t-5\times10^{-2}x)\,\text{V/m}\). Find the maximum magnetic force. (\(c=3\times10^{8}\), \(e=1.6\times10^{-19}\,\text{C}\))

▸ Solution

First \(B_0=E_0/c\), then \(F_{\max}=q\,(0.1c)\,B_0=q(0.1c)\dfrac{E_0}{c}=0.1\,qE_0\):

\[F_{\max}=0.1\,(1.6\times10^{-19})(30)=4.8\times10^{-19}\,\text{N}\]

Answer (1)   \(4.8\times10^{-19}\,\text{N}\)
05

Motion in parallel \(\vec E\) and \(\vec B\) — speed doubling

JEE Main 2020

Skill chain: magnetic force does no work → \(v_x=qE_0t/m\) → perpendicular speed stays \(v_0\) → Pythagoras for “speed doubles.”

Fig 5 · \(B\) rotates the \(y\)–\(z\) motion (constant \(v_0\)); \(E\) accelerates along \(x\), stretching the helix.
L1Easy

Does the magnetic force change the speed (kinetic energy) of a moving charge? Why?

▸ Solution

No. \(\vec F_{\text{mag}}=q\vec v\times\vec B\) is always perpendicular to \(\vec v\), so it does zero work.

Speed is unchanged by \(B\) alone
L2Warm-up

A particle (mass \(m\), charge \(q\)) starts from rest in a uniform field \(\vec E=E_0\hat i\). Find its speed after time \(t\).

▸ Solution

\(a=\dfrac{qE_0}{m}\), so \(v=at\).

\(v=\dfrac{qE_0 t}{m}\)
L3Core

A particle has velocity \(v_0\hat j\) when \(\vec B=B_0\hat i\) acts. Explain why the \(y\)–\(z\) speed stays \(v_0\); if it also gains \(v_x\), write the total speed.

▸ Solution

\(B\) along \(x\) only rotates the \((y,z)\) velocity — its magnitude \(v_0\) is conserved. The \(x\)-motion is independent, so

total speed \(=\sqrt{v_x^{2}+v_0^{2}}\)
L4Challenge

The perpendicular speed stays \(v_0\) while \(\vec E=E_0\hat i\) gives \(v_x=\dfrac{qE_0 t}{m}\). Find the time when the total speed becomes \(2v_0\).

▸ Solution

\[(2v_0)^2=v_x^2+v_0^2\Rightarrow v_x^2=3v_0^2\Rightarrow \frac{qE_0t}{m}=\sqrt3\,v_0\]

\(t=\dfrac{\sqrt3\,m v_0}{qE_0}\)
L5Original · JEE Main 2020

A particle of mass \(m\), charge \(q\) has \(\vec v=v_0\hat j\). With \(\vec E=E_0\hat i\) and \(\vec B=B_0\hat i\) acting, its speed doubles after a time:

▸ Solution

\(\vec E\parallel\vec B\parallel\hat i\). \(B\) does no work and keeps the perpendicular speed at \(v_0\); \(E\) accelerates along \(x\): \(v_x=\dfrac{qE_0t}{m}\). Speed doubles when

\[\sqrt{v_x^2+v_0^2}=2v_0\Rightarrow v_x=\sqrt3\,v_0\Rightarrow t=\frac{\sqrt3\,m v_0}{qE_0}\]

Answer (2)   \(\dfrac{\sqrt3\,m v_0}{qE_0}\)
